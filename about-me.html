About Me

Hi! I am Char Juin, a sophomore at University of California, Berkeley! I am studying for my BA in Computer Science and Linguistics, with potentially a Data Science minor as well.

I am also working as a research apprentice with the university's Linguistics Research Apprenticeship Practicum (LRAP). Together with my research mentor, Schuyler Laparle, we are studying the use of co-speech gestures in structuring discourse during conversation.

I have completed two summer internships with Singapore Airlines (SIA). In 2020, I worked with their Ground Experience Development team to develop new back-end systems in support of customer interactions, such as cabin crew and customer feedback handling, and in deployment of an emergency pandemic assistance form. In 2021, I worked with their Data and Analytics team to predict the adoption rates of a new e-Commerce platform by existing SIA customers.

My passion lies in the field of Natural Language Processing, in which I have published a paper in the IEEE TENCON 2017 conference. I am especially interested in machine translation, semantic analysis, and speech processing. In the future, I hope to develop technologies that can bridge the cultural gap between people and improve communication between people.

I am bilingual in English and Chinese, am currently self-studying Japanese, and taking also French classes at university.

To exercise, I enjoy running and hiking, having had the opportunity to travel to Nepal in February 2020 right before the pandemic struck! In my free time, I enjoy brewing coffee, playing the guitar, video games, and reading manga.
